# 王建硕 — 著作与系统性思考 (Writings & Systematic Thinking)

**Source basis:** First-hand material — Wang Jianshuo's own English-language blog essays
(wangjianshuo.com, 2002–2013), as collected in `corpus_longform.txt` (217 essays ≥800
words). Every claim below cites the post by **date + title**. The corpus is his own
writing, so these are his actual words and positions, not second-hand characterization.

Notational convention used throughout:
- **他直接说的** — paraphrase/quote of an explicit statement.
- **[推断]** — an inference I draw from patterns in the text; flagged explicitly.

A note on the man's own epistemic style: he constantly hedges ("this is my CURRENT point
of view," "I am a blind man," "correct me if I am wrong"). Any "core belief" listed below
is held *by him* in exactly that provisional spirit — he would be the first to add a
disclaimer. I preserve his contradictions rather than smoothing them.

---

## 1. Recurring Core Beliefs (expressed 3+ times across different posts/topics)

### 1.1 "I never write down something I know is not true." — the one hard rule of the blog
This is the single most-repeated principle in the entire corpus. He states it as *the*
rule, verbatim, again and again:
- **2005-11-07 | BBC's Interview**: "it is always right to be truthful to my heart and do
  not put anything that I personally don't believe in this blog. This is the rule."
- **2006-12-02 | The Blind Men and The Elephant**: "here is the rule I used to write my
  blog: I don't write down something I know is not true."
- **2008-04-06 | Why I Didn't Cover About Tibet**: "As one rule I setup from the day one
  of this blog five years ago, I only want to post something I personal experience and I
  never post something that I know it is not true."
- Also surfaces in **2002-09-17 | I was a Called Copier Today** (in embryonic form — the
  credibility/give-credit episode that seeded his ethics of honest sourcing).

Corollary he draws explicitly: even reporting a newspaper rumor is "true" *as a record
that this rumor reached a normal person in Shanghai* — "Isn't it also a valuable piece of
history record?" (**2006-12-02 | The Blind Men and The Elephant**).

### 1.2 "I am a blind man" — radical epistemic humility; nobody sees the whole elephant
His master metaphor (see §2.1). The belief: every person, including himself, only ever
touches part of reality and part of history; therefore strong, totalizing claims are
almost always wrong.
- **2005-11-07 | BBC's Interview**: "My favorate story is the 'blind man and the
  elephant'. We are all the blind man... What I don't see is what I don't see."
- **2006-12-02 | The Blind Men and The Elephant**: entire essay; "Since I don't have the
  confidence as many of my strong-minded commentors to claim I am the person who knows
  the whole world best, I can only write my own observation."
- **2007-09-23 | Don't Leave, AussiePB**: "We are just blind men in this world. No matter
  how ridiculous or naive... the other person is saying, it may be one angle for the same
  thing."
- **2011-10-29 | Reflection of My 5 Years of YLF**: re-uses it as the official frame for
  US–China dialogue.
- The disclaimers on nearly every opinion piece ("this only represents my CURRENT point
  of view... any one's POV may change," **2006-06-20 | One Child Policy**) are the
  operational form of this belief.

### 1.3 "Keep doing, and doing, and doing" — accumulation beats talent or willpower
A genuine conviction, repeated as a life philosophy and tested on his own blog.
- **2005-01-29 | Keep Doing, and Doing, and Doing**: "If something is meaningful, go
  ahead to do it. If time is not the key factor, just keep doing, and doing, and doing...
  The result is just there." (The balloon-string story is the parable.)
- **2006-05-11 | Keep Doing, and Its Meaning**: refines it — it's *not* about
  "consistency" (firmness of mind); "I don't think I am a person of consistency." It's
  about whether you still find the thing **meaningful**. "As long as the reason is still
  valid... you keep doing."
- **2006-03-09 | Hot Articles in 2002 to 2005**: "It is the demonstration of how much we
  can achieve with daily accumulation - 15 minutes a day means a lot after three years."
- **2007-09-11 | Five Years of Blogging**: "Blogging is the first thing in my life that I
  intentionally continued to do (besides sleeping, and eating) for so long."
- He credits the seed idea to **Wu Hao** ("who gave me the suggestion to accumulate
  something along the way").

### 1.4 "Seek first to understand, then to be understood" — communication over confrontation
A constant. He explicitly attributes the phrasing to this maxim (Covey's, though he
doesn't always name it).
- **2008-04-11 | Friends Started to Boycott French Products**: "Guided with my approach
  to the world: 'Seek first to understand, then to understood.'"
- **2008-04-09 | More Discussion on Tibet**: "Understand first, then talk... People who
  don't want to understand others are often the people who think they are not understood."
- **2003-04-08 | SARS: Culture Shock?**: early version — "lack of information and mutal
  understanding causes fear."
- Pairs with his self-identified role as **"a bridge"** between East and West (see §2.2).

### 1.5 The world is not black and white; disagreeing with one side ≠ supporting the other
- **2008-04-24 | Mixing, Muddling, and Confusing**: "Disagree with One Side in a Debate
  does not Mean Support the Other Side... It is 'you' who think I am implying something,
  not me." Quotes reader George W. Shen at length: "THE WORLD ISN'T JUST BLACK AND WHITE."
- **2008-04-09 | More Discussion on Tibet**: "This is not a Two-Sided World. This is
  multi-sided world... never assume I am on one side or the other... I am trying to be on
  the side of wisdom, truth, and rational."
- **2008-04-23 | Four Types of Consciousness**: builds a whole gray-scale taxonomy
  (Angry Youth / Conscious Patriot / God-like Analyst / Careful Balancer) precisely to
  refuse the two-camp framing.
- **2008-04-23 | Grace Wang Called a Traitor**: "I try to avoid simply saying: 'I am on
  this side or the other side.'"

### 1.6 Don't just identify problems — solve them; complainers vs. contributors
- **2008-04-13 | Not Just Identify Problems for China - Solve Them!**: title is the
  thesis. "Young Generations like myself tend to be able to identify a problem, but often
  fail to solve the problem... I need to do something to HELP SOLVING the problem."
- **2006-06-20 | One Child Policy in China**: "I always tell my employees who are
  complaining about one problem or another, that when they're done complaining, they
  should try to be productive and suggest solutions."
- **2005-03-01 | Helping by Hiring**: "I don't like to just talk about something without
  action." His whole "Helping by Hiring" argument is action-over-rhetoric.
- **2006-01-02 | I Don't Drive Well After Back from San Jose**: "If I have the power to
  change the rule, I change the rule... If I cannot change the rule, I will follow the
  EXISTING rule instead of making trouble."

### 1.7 Patriotism-as-ownership: "I share the shame of the country as well as the glory"
He loves China *as a part-owner who must fix it*, explicitly contrasted with foreigners
who can simply "point fingers."
- **2006-06-20 | One Child Policy in China**: "I share the shame of the country as well
  as the glory. I am embarrassed to see the mistakes the government made... To say 'I
  hate China' or 'I am out of here' is easy for many people, if they are not part of the
  country. But it is not possible for me... we are the people to solve it, right?"
- **2008-04-13 | Not Just Identify Problems for China**: "We are the people who need to
  contribute to a better China, instead of just complainer, like those outside the
  country."
- **2008-04-16 | Real Pictures of China with My Experience**: "we need to reflect as a
  nation about who pulled the rich country in the past into the bottom of poverty and
  chaos" — criticism and pride coexist in the same sentence.

### 1.8 Anti-hate / anti-boycott — hate is an infectious disease
- **2006-11-07 | I am not a Big Fan of Hate - Part II**: "hate spread like virus, and
  people believe by hating other countries, they demonstrate patriotism... If you ask me,
  I would stand out and stop the hate."
- **2009-08-07 | My Passion for Humanity**: "Crime is like Infectious Disease... Hate, for
  example, is very infectious." Develops the full disease model — the criminal is a
  patient, not a different species.
- **2008-04-11 | Friends Started to Boycott French Products**: "I believe boycotting is an
  immature way to handle problems. It works only when you want to create more problems."
- **2009-08-07** explicitly links to his Chinese post "The Tragedy of Humanity, and Love
  for the Guilty."

### 1.9 Personal connection is irreplaceable; news without it is meaningless
- **2006-06-29 | NCUCR Visit**: "I am a strong believer of personal connections. Nothing
  can replace it... 'What does it mean for me if one delegate visits this city?'"
- **2011-10-29 | Reflection of My 5 Years of YLF**: "I am a strong believer of personal
  connections. It cannot be replaced, even by social media... I started to care so much
  when the friend I made here, Chris, is on that shuttle."
- Reframed in **2006-06-29**: impact is "not quantitatively significant, but
  qualitatively significant" — helping even one person counts.

### 1.10 Helping one or two people is enough — no ambition to "save the world"
- **2006-06-20 | One Child Policy in China**: "I have no interest or ambition to save the
  world. I am only interested in and enjoy about helping just one person or two persons.
  That is good enough for me."
- **2005-03-01 | Helping by Hiring**: "One personal cannot make an impact to the world,
  but at least can help one or two other people. Help people one by one."
- **2006-06-29 | NCUCR Visit**: revises an earlier marketing-manager instinct ("any
  program need to cover at least 1,000 people") — "Recently, I found I was wrong. If a
  program can impact even one person, it makes difference."

### 1.11 China changes — but slower than outsiders demand; give it patience
- **2009-05-18 | Is China Changeable?**: "in all standard, China IS changing, if you put
  China into a scale of 30 years. However, it is NOT as fast as expected."
- **2006-06-20 | One Child Policy in China**: the SimCity parable — "Everyone knows a much
  better solution, but that solution has a condition, that people need to get started to
  build the conditions first... give the country more patience."
- **2013-05-30 | Why eBay Failed in China?**: same patience theme applied to business —
  "Lack of patience is one of the key reason they failed."

### 1.12 Happiness does not depend on the things people chase
- **2005-03-05 | What does Happiness Depend on**: quotes de Botton/Wordsworth — cities
  foster "a family of life-destroying emotions"; "our happiness did not depend on those
  fancy stuff."
- **2005-09-07 | We Filled Our Lives But Lost Our Souls**: amplifies Wendy's essay — "We
  filled our lives, but we lost our souls."
- **2011-07-02 / 2011-07-03 | Why I Won't / WILL Return my MacBook Air**: extended,
  almost philosophical treatment via Daniel Gilbert — "People don't know what makes them
  happy eventually"; "the desire for the better" is examined as a trap.
- His and Wendy's persistent anti-materialism is documented as lived practice:
  **2009-09-13 | Bought a TCL Internet TV** ("we are never too materialism to buy big TV,
  big car"), the 5-year-old Fiat joke, the small TV kept for years.

## 2. Self-Coined Concepts / Signature Framings

### 2.1 "The Blind Men and the Elephant" — his single governing metaphor
Not coined by him, but he *adopts and rewrites* it as a personal epistemology and even
authored his own poem version, **"The Six Blind Men and China"** (**2006-12-02 | The
Blind Men and The Elephant**), "based on the work of John Godfrey Saxe." It is the
backbone of his thinking about China, media, comments, and US–China relations. Used in at
least: 2005-11-07, 2006-12-02, 2007-09-23, 2008-04-09 (implied), 2011-10-29.

### 2.2 "A bridge" between the West and the East (and "many worlds")
His self-defined *purpose* as a blogger. He frames himself as "a little bridge."
- **2007-09-11 | Five Years of Blogging**: "As a Chinese blogger writing in English, I am
  happy to act as a little bridge between the west and the east."
- **2008-04-09 | More Discussion on Tibet**: "I always see myself and my little blog as a
  bridge between the two worlds (in fact, many worlds)."
- **2006-06-29 | NCUCR Visit**: "I just said 'we need a bridge' one week ago. Today, I saw
  one of the bridges." (Note the YLF logo literally being a bridge — **2011-10-29**.)

### 2.3 "It is not impossible, but difficult" — his frame for how China works
A signature explanatory device. **2006-05-08 | It is not Impossible, But Difficult**:
"In China, the culture is not based on philosophy, reasoning or mathematics... U.S. rules
works more like mathematics; China rules more works like human being." China-as-art vs.
West-as-equation. "If you don't understand what 'It is not impossible, but it is
difficult' means, you don't really understand China yet." He created a blog category,
**`westmeetseast`**, for this whole theme.

### 2.4 "The world is not created by genius" — the 2/3-of-the-average game
A self-developed parable (**2008-05-31 | The World is Not Created by Genius - Part II**,
building on an earlier "Part I"). Players guess a number; winner is closest to 2/3 of the
average; the winning number is always ~22. His taxonomy of people by their guess:
- **67+ = random voters** — don't think, don't care, but still shape the outcome.
- **33 = the vast majority** — follow rules, never push further; "the baseline of the
  world."
- **22 = the winners** — understand the rule and go *exactly one step* further; "they
  helped many 33s to improve life by one step."
- **0 = geniuses** — "genius inside but stupid outside"; right about the world, wrong
  about people; "you are a genius, but you cannot and will never win the game." Bruno,
  Galileo invoked.
Thesis: progress is driven by the *one-step-ahead* 22s, not the geniuses; "the world is
not created by genius." [推断] This doubles as his theory of his own positioning — he
aspires to be a 22, a pragmatic one-step-ahead communicator, not a martyr-genius.

### 2.5 "Four Types of Consciousness" — a gray-scale of political actors
**2008-04-23 | Four Types of Consciousness**: a coined four-bucket taxonomy along a
"consciousness/rationality" axis, applied symmetrically to both Chinese and Western
camps:
1. **Angry Youth** — simple-minded, ready to die/kill on command.
2. **Conscious Patriot** — act deliberately for their country (only their country).
3. **God-like Analyst** — call for calm, communication, solutions; "called traitors in
   any camp"; "their voice is seldom heard."
4. **Careful Balancer** — governments; must perform anger while not crossing the line.
He places himself fluidly: "I used to be the Angry Youth... I sometimes act as 'Conscious
Patriot', and often, I am God-like Analyst (like in this post)."

### 2.6 "Enjoy doing" vs. "enjoy being able to do"
**2005-03-07 | Enjoy Doing or Being Able to Do**: a coined distinction. People buy gym
memberships, apartments near sports facilities, live in cultured cities — and never use
any of it. "We paid for 'being able to exercise at anytime.'" Connected to his
"Shanghai tour for Shanghainese" idea and his crazy projects (all Starbucks in a day;
visiting confluence point 30N 119E). Related companion posts he names: "curiosity for
life," "dislike doing or dislike starting."

### 2.7 "Three dreams" framing of Chinese mobility
**2005-02-01 | Do Chinese Move to Small Cities**: structures the whole essay as nested
unreachable dreams — moving *into* cities is a dream; moving into the *biggest* cities is
a dream; moving *out* to smaller cities is "also a dream that no one takes it seriously."
Uses the idiom **背井离乡 (Bei Jing Li Xiang)** as the cultural anchor.

### 2.8 "Genius inside but stupid outside" / "the fate of genius"
See 2.4 — a recurring sub-frame: technical brilliance ≠ understanding people, and
popularity-judged systems (democracy, markets) punish the genius.

### 2.9 Business heuristics he coined for entrepreneurs (**2010-01-03 | My Advice to Entrepreneurs**)
- **"Try to use bad words to describe your business"** — a winning business has *one* good
  word and many bad ones ("the fastest websites, although it is ugly, lack of function,
  and cost more"). "There are many good things in this world, but you can only pick one
  or two at most at the same time."
- **"A business model fits revenue and cost"**; **"align product and business"** (the two
  arrows pointing the same direction); **"there is demand does not mean you can do it
  well."**

### 2.10 "China is different only in one way: it is too big"
**2013-05-30 | Why eBay Failed in China?**: explicitly rejects the lazy "China is
different" explanation. The real variable is *scale* — China is big enough that building
a marginally-better local competitor is economically justified ("the so-what question has
an answer"), which is not true in Netherlands. Plus "the composition of value": Internet
companies carry little embedded tech value, so they arrive at "a leveling playing field
with no advantages." And "swinging from over-optimistic to over-pessimistic and never
swing back."

### 2.11 "Be personal is more important than focus"
**2004-07-23 | To Focus or Not?**: against the universal marketing advice to "focus." For
a *personal* blog, "to be personal is more meaningful for both readers and the writer."
Final formula: "keep a focus with strong personal presence."

## 3. His Philosophy of Blogging & Writing

He wrote an unusual amount of meta-commentary; blogging milestones (1 year, 1000 days, 3
years, 5 years, 8 years) are recurring reflective set-pieces.

### What writing/blogging is FOR
- **Primarily for himself — accumulation.** **2006-05-11 | Keep Doing, and Its Meaning**:
  "Only after I realized to write for myself, instead of for visitors, and page view... I
  found the reason to keep blogging." "to keep accumulating my thoughts on daily bases.
  It may be a big book one day." The order is explicit: "at first, I write for myself, to
  accumulate my life experience. Secondly, if it can help someone out there... that is
  better."
- **A thinking tool.** **2007-09-11 | Five Years of Blogging**: "The blog is the tool to
  help me think, and to urge me observe more deeply, and express it in a logic way."
- **A record of an ordinary life as history.** **2006-12-02 | The Blind Men and The
  Elephant**: a normal person's daily life is "a valuable piece of history record."
  Invokes *The Diary of Anne Frank* as the model — "general perception is different than
  REAL people's life."
- **To add value by first-hand experience, not by repeating news.** **2008-04-06 | Why I
  Didn't Cover About Tibet**: "this blog is not a media. I want to add value by telling
  people what I, this person, see, and experience, instead of repeating what I read."
- **A bridge for cross-cultural understanding** (see §2.2).
- **It is "all about ME and YOUR world."** **2004-07-17 | To Continue or Not?**:
  "blogging is about YOU, not a topic... the ONLY place in this whole universe that
  clearly expresses you." Explicitly: it is **NOT a diary** ("Diary is the secret log for
  myself... It is NOT a diary and don't feel guilty if you cannot face yourself in the
  most frank way").

### Rules he holds himself to
1. **Never write something you know is not true** (§1.1) — the master rule.
2. **Write daily / keep the heartbeat.** **2004-07-17**: "daily update (at least two day
   update) as my criteria for blogging." **2006-11-23 | Rumors after Rumors II**: "a blog
   is all about life, and it is like the pulse of heart-beat... If you quit for one day or
   two, it is not easy to pickup." He posts "OOB" (Out of Blogging) notes rather than go
   silent.
3. **Never delete a comment** (unless spam or unreadable). **2007-09-23 | Don't Leave,
   AussiePB**: "I have a strict policy on this blog that I do NOT delete any comment...
   no matter how convenient this action is, it is against the spirit of free speech... I
   am not 100% confident about my own judgment of what is right or what is wrong."
4. **No personal attacks allowed** (the one thing he *will* moderate). Same post; also
   2006-11-07.
5. **Tolerance / ignore-and-move-on** as the survival tool for readers in conflict
   (2007-09-23).
6. **Give credit; link back.** Seeded in **2002-09-17 | I was a Called Copier Today** —
   "to show respect to others by giving others credit. This is the most easy and powerful
   way to build up my own credit."
7. **Heavy use of disclaimers** — "this is my CURRENT point of view," "I am not a
   spokesperson," "correct me if I am wrong" (2006-06-20 and passim). He admits he hates
   writing them ("The Disclaimer (I hate it but I have to)") but treats them as
   intellectually obligatory.
8. **Separate the blogging world from the business world** — an intention he states
   repeatedly while admitting "the two worlds run into each other" because "it is all
   about me" (**2006-11-23 | Rumors after Rumors II**).
9. **The writer is the most important reader.** "I am still the most important reader of
   this blog. Everything I write — at least it should look interesting to me." (2006-11-23,
   quoting his own 4-year anniversary wishes.)

### Influences on his blogging practice specifically
- **Mark Pilgrim's "Mark's 10 Tips on Writing the Living Web"** — "my favorite guideline
  on blogging. I often review the guidelines." Lists all 10 (Write for a reason / Write
  often / Write tight / Make good friends / Find good enemies / Let the story unfold /
  Stand up, speak out / Be sexy / Use your archive / Relax!) — **2004-07-17 | To Continue
  or Not?**
- **Geo (russianlegs.com)** introduced him to the weblog form; **andersja's blog**
  inspired his early design and the categories feature; **MovableType / Six Apart team**
  (Ben Trott, Mena Trott, Anil Dash, Ginger, Barak) — "I am inspired by the team"
  (2002-09-11, 2007-09-11).
- **Brett (WebmasterWorld), "Successful Site in 12 Months with Google Alone"** — quoted in
  2003-03-25 | Inside Wangjianshuo.com II.
- **Wu Hao** — gave him the "accumulate something along the way" idea (2007-09-11).
- **Dan Washburn (TIME)** — taught him "everything is 'on the record' in western media"
  (2005-11-07).

## 4. Worldview Themes

### China's development
- **Genuinely optimistic, on a 30-year time scale; impatient outsiders mistake slowness
  for stasis.** §1.11. "I am even more confidence about the future of China" *after*
  losing his naïve trust (2008-04-13).
- **Mistakes compound: many of China's problems were created by solving earlier
  problems** (over-population → one-child policy → aging society). **2006-06-20 | One
  Child Policy in China**: "more problems were created by solving those old problems."
- **The collapse-of-trust experience is generational and necessary.** **2008-04-13**:
  "The collapse of value system after knowing many truth will be the main theme for many
  people in the next decade in China. I experience that during my month long cross North
  American trip in the winter of 2004." It took him "four years" to "feel pretty calm now."
- **China's continuity (4000 years, united since 221 BC) explains why Chinese treasure a
  united country** — and why outsiders misjudge it. **2008-04-06 | Why I Didn't Cover
  About Tibet**: the "price of being united" argument (US paid one civil war; China paid
  "thousands of wars").
- **Mega-projects pride coexists with awareness of tax-money abuse** (2008-04-24, refers
  to his "Mega-Projects" post). The "right to enter a train from the door is also part of
  human right" (**2008-04-16 | Real Pictures of China**) — material progress is real
  progress, even as he insists on political/speech rights.
- He calls the **Great Firewall "a shame in our country"** (2008-04-13) and proudly
  archives his "sensitive" posts; his own blog gets intermittently banned.

### Optimism vs. pessimism (a real, preserved tension)
- Default stance is optimistic: "human being has survived by overcoming one challenge
  after the other, and so does a country" (2006-06-20).
- But he documents his own swings: the 2004 North America trip turned him "very negative
  for some time" (2008-04-13); the 2010 internet crackdown was "depressing," "I Don't
  Like 2010" (2010-12-31). He does not hide the down years.
- He critiques the *structural* over-optimism→over-pessimism swing in others
  (multinational Internet companies, 2013-05-30) and prescribes the middle: "they just
  need to be realistic."

### Individual vs. system
- **Recurring, unresolved tension.** **2006-01-02 | I Don't Drive Well After Back from
  San Jose**: the most honest statement of it — following the "right" traffic rule alone
  makes you "a trouble maker"; "every society has its own rule. If I have the power to
  change the rule, I change the rule... If I cannot change the rule, I will follow the
  EXISTING rule instead of making trouble." He explicitly extends this to doing business
  in China and confesses "What a wired answer I have."
- **2009-05-18 | Is China Changeable?**: the Shanghai Jewish refugees as the emblem of
  "individual's fight with bigger system" — refuses to call survival cowardice. Long-term
  goal (change the system) vs. short-term goal (his son Yifan's education) "does not fit."
- **2009-08-21 | What if Someone Cut my Tree?**: bleak legal system → "many years of
  social experience tend to teach people to protect themselves by themselves"; yet "I
  still keep the hope... as long as there is still a place to talk about the legal
  system."
- **2012-08-03 | Reading Note on Richard Hamming's**: adopts Hamming's verdict — "you
  cannot do the two things together: change the system or be a first-class scientist...
  try to work with the system instead of fight against." But qualifies: "when there is a
  universal principle, stick to it, but there are some local principles, and we should
  respect."

### Cross-cultural understanding
- The central mission (see §1.4, §2.2). West = mathematics/logic/Yes-No; East =
  art/harmony/ambiguity (**2006-05-08**). He insists this is *descriptive*, not a ranking
  ("It does hurt efficiency, I agree, but it is the reason why the culture lasted
  thousands of years").
- **Generalization is the core logical error** in cross-cultural talk. **2008-05-01 |
  Generalization Plays the Trick**: adding "*some of*" fixes the "to the western world"
  poem; "be very careful to use the word of 'China', or 'western world', or 'France'."
- Distrusts *all* media symmetrically — CCTV/local propaganda *and* BBC/CNN. **2005-11-07
  | BBC's Interview**: "BBC interview = censorship question interview"; refuses to be "a
  victim in the story." **2008-04-06**: "I don't believe in CNN... I believed 19 years
  ago — at that time, we only have Voice of America. But it turned out that many of the
  news are biased." (Note the 1989 reference, handled obliquely.)
- "To report the fact does not always mean to report the truth" — agenda-setting itself
  filters (2005-11-07).

### Family
- **2007-09-11 | Five Years of Blogging**: "officially" thanks Wendy for tolerating the
  blogging habit ("that is the time most people watch TV... with wife. I know this
  blogging stole much time from Wendy").
- Wendy functions in the corpus as his **moral corrective**: **2009-08-05 | I am a Rich
  Person?** — "'Jian Shuo, how can you be so mean to them?'... Wendy helped me to stay
  humble and grateful to this world, and don't be the King of the Universe."
- **2008-06-04 | Review at Yifan's 1st Birthday**: birthdays exist "for everyone to take
  the time to celebrate the milestone, and take serious time to review the past year." —
  reflection as a designed ritual.
- Yifan's education becomes a multi-post agony (2009-05-17 through 2009-05-22) — the
  sharpest case of his individual-vs-system tension lived at home.

### Money / wealth
- **Anti-materialist as lived practice** (5-year-old Fiat, small TV, no big bags;
  2009-09-13). "we are never too materialism."
- **2009-08-05 | I am a Rich Person?** — his most uncomfortable, honest essay: he
  realizes that by his own behavior ("so arrogant, and so self-centered") he has become

## 6. Preserved Contradictions & Tensions (do not smooth these)

1. **"I don't like talking" / writes 3000+ posts.** He repeatedly says he dislikes mere
   talk and prefers action (2006-06-20: "you will understand I don't like talking"), yet
   his life's signature artifact is a decade of daily writing. He resolves it by claiming
   the blog *is* action — a bridge — but the tension is real and he half-acknowledges it.
2. **Radical relativism vs. firm verdicts.** He insists "I try to avoid simply saying I
   am on this side or the other," yet in the same period delivers hard judgments: "people
   in Paris did something wrong" (2008-04-11), "I don't like Google now" (2006-11-23 P.S.),
   boycotting "is an immature way." His relativism is about *framing*, not about having no
   opinions — but the line wobbles.
3. **Optimism vs. documented despair.** §4. The stated optimism is sincere; so are "I
   Don't Like 2010" and "very negative for some time."
4. **Anti-materialism vs. the MacBook Air saga.** Two long essays agonizing over a laptop
   purchase, by the man who keeps a 5-year-old Fiat and a small TV "to be low-cost as
   possible." He *uses* the contradiction as material — examining "the desire for the
   better" in himself.
5. **Patriotic pride vs. "I am embarrassed to see the mistakes."** Held deliberately in
   the same breath (§1.7) — he treats this not as a contradiction but as what mature
   ownership feels like.
6. **"Follow the existing rule" pragmatism vs. "if I have the power, I change the rule"
   reformism.** 2006-01-02 and 2012-08-03 lean pragmatic ("work with the system");
   2009-05-18 and the GFW comments lean reformist. He never fully reconciles it; he
   carves out "universal principle, stick to it; local principle, respect it" as the
   uneasy seam.
7. **"Write for myself" vs. deep dependence on readers.** He says page views don't matter
   and he is "the most important reader," yet calls readers "the biggest wealth in my
   life," relies on comments as his epistemic correction mechanism (the blind men need
   each other), and is visibly hurt when a reader leaves (2007-09-23).

## 7. One-line synthesis for the next phase

Wang Jianshuo's systematic thinking is a **pragmatic epistemology of humility in action**:
*I can only see part of the elephant, so I will write only what I have personally
verified, every day, for myself first; I will seek to understand before I judge; I will
be a one-step-ahead bridge rather than a martyr-genius or an angry complainer; and I will
help one or two real people, patiently, instead of pronouncing on the whole world.* Its
recurring tools are the blind-men metaphor, the West-as-math/East-as-art frame, the
"keep doing" accumulation ethic, and the refusal of black-and-white, two-camp,
generalized thinking.
