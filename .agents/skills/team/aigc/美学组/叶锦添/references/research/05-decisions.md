# 05 决策记录与行动模式

## Decision: Enter cinema through art direction, then expand outward

- 1986: art director for John Woo's `A Better Tomorrow`; followed by major film collaborations with Stanley Kwan, Clara Law, Ang Lee, Tsai Ming-liang, Chen Kaige, Feng Xiaogang, Li Shaohong, Wuershan, and others.
  - Source: https://www.timyipstudio.com/content/biography/en
  - Inference: he used cinema as a base discipline but did not stay inside film.

## Decision: Use `Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon` as an international translation point

- 2000/2001: production and costume design for Ang Lee's `Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon`; won Academy Award for Best Art Direction and BAFTA Costume Design.
  - Sources: https://www.timyipstudio.com/content/biography/en and https://www.bafta.org/awards/film/costume-design/
  - Pattern: martial arts, landscape, costume, and emotional restraint became a global visual language without simply copying period realism.

## Decision: Name and keep developing `New Oriental`

- Since 2002, he introduced a `New Oriental` aesthetic.
  - Source: https://www.akramkhancompany.net/company-profiles/1016/
  - Pattern: the act of naming the theory turned scattered work into a portable method.

## Decision: Keep Lili as a long-term private/public medium

- `Silent Passenger` and later `Mirror Universe` show Lili as repeated figure, sculpture, photograph, video, projection, and AI/future-facing entity.
  - Sources: https://www.timyipstudio.com/content/article/en/49 and https://www.sohu.com/a/911555798_121106875
  - Pattern: instead of constantly inventing new icons, he deepens one vessel across contexts.

## Decision: Move Chinese visuality into global institutional stages

- Worked with Met Opera `Lohengrin`, San Francisco Opera `Dream of the Red Chamber`, Akram Khan/English National Ballet, Hong Kong Ballet, Minneapolis Institute of Art, Olympics podium uniforms.
  - Sources: https://www.timyipstudio.com/content/biography/en and China Daily `Stage presence`.
  - Pattern: design not as isolated artifact but as cultural interface in high-pressure global venues.
