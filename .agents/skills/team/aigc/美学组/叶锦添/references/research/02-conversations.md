# 02 对话、访谈与即兴思考

## China Daily: Met Opera `Lohengrin`

- Yip says the Met project required months of studying Wagner's story and music, including viewing older Met versions.
  - Source: https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202303/06/WS64052ea8a31057c47ebb25fe_2.html
  - Confidence: high; direct interview.
- He states that design choices should prioritize Wagner's music; color, light, shadow, choreography, and space all need rhythm.
  - Source: same.
  - Pattern: he does not begin from surface style; he lets the originating discipline set the design grammar.
- For `Eternal Offerings`, he wanted a multidimensional way to see ancient bronzes through immersive backdrop, sound, projection, and light.
  - Source: https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202303/06/WS64052ea8a31057c47ebb25fe_3.html
  - Pattern: antiquity is reactivated through new perceptual apparatus.

## Phoenix Weekly Life / `Mirror Universe`

- Yip links `Mirror Universe` to inner/outer unfamiliarity, time-space questions, AI recombination, and a search for an abstract medium beyond simple explanation.
  - Source: https://www.sohu.com/a/911555798_121106875
  - Confidence: medium; reposted interview.
- He uses Lili as a medium of `zero time`: a figure who can stay fixed while moving through many places, memories, and projected futures.
  - Source: same.
  - Pattern: character design becomes time design.
- He resists giving fixed answers inside the exhibition; ambiguity and viewer projection are part of the work.
  - Source: same.
  - Pattern: leave interpretive space rather than closing the symbol.

## Conversation Signals

- He often answers through abstract relations: time/space, spirit/body, inside/outside, visible/invisible, ancient/future.
- He does not treat tradition as a museum label; he asks what ancient material can do to today's perception.
- His strongest design statements appear when a concrete production problem meets a metaphysical question.
